sometimes when my mind wanders i think of old friendships that ended in silence


when i think of you i’m taken back to the field behind the private school by your house, on the same
road as the pizza place where i got my first job and the house with the basement shows where i had
my first kiss

the sun is always setting (though i know in truth it was probably beating down mostly) and i’m cold
but not too cold and just insecure enough to run until my lungs are dry and empty in a no-stakes
winner-takes-all game of touch football

we never got to finish

i’m reminded of the sensation of my nails against a coarse, alien landscape, like a craggy mountain
valley or an autumn leaf left to dry up in winter. i scratch my scabs until the goosebumps on my
knees are red and don’t rip them off until one side tears and i can feel it pressing up against my skin,
like an old patch three-quarters-sewn onto a jacket
