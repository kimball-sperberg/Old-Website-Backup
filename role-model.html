role model

the first time we were tripping i stared at the sun with my eyes closed
          thoughts and images of steel boxes, rusted train tracks
                    & concrete colored clouds, looming,
                              flashing in infinite regression
                                       past the horizon,
                                        into oblivion,  behind my eyelids
                                                            i swam downward, inward, like a child
                                                                      eyes open underwater
                                                                                for the first time
                                                                                          seeing the abyss below me
                                                                                                    spiraling
your voice pulled me out
          & sent me crashing down onto the hot red rocks  like
                                                                                             gravity you were the leader naturally not self-appointed, like me and it was time to go so i trudged forward, carrying a burden of resentment i could not place, nor confront & departed the cliff in a web of my own creation we took the long way out of the woods that day joked that we were forest people and vowed never to join the army when the dry leaf got caught in the wind, sun was dripping through a crack in the overhang i missed your gaze, and we both looked down before i looked back up, you were gone memetic tremors from that band we saw echo out, enclosing me reverberating off cloudy fiberglass walls pounding down like raindrops on dragonfly wings & steely riverbeds on my scalp i said i’d be there, so i’ll come & let you in disingenuousness tasted sour, lingering like untouched potatoes, months old on the counter proliferating an open residence to fungus, churning unnoticed unfolding an array of filth devoid of intention & left to its own accord dead water drips from the old kitchen sink chemical residue trickles down my spine submerges me flooding all my caverns & perforations overtaking, washing over each and every vacuole of my swollen honeycomb tongue it dribbles down my copper throat when i swallow, erupting, spilling over into waves of neon & krylon fractals in my head it captures me & takes me home tennessee mud caked between my toes i felt like a willow in the storm’s eye static & brittle in my head & my surroundings inaction offered quiet relief but soon enough restlessness stirred & naturally, all eyes turned to you & naturally, mine did too and in that moment i caught a glimpse of your truth it shouldn’t take a genius to see we all have our own insecurities i guess somewhere along the line i forgot to lump you in it’s a shame it took me seeing you crack for me to know you weren’t against me You said, “Someone else choose, I fucking hate leading”
